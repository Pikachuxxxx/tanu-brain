Tanu is a fascinating character. She's defined by her transition from being "trapped" in a cassette tape head (a metaphor for being stuck in a loop or a static past) to finding her "real self" in a shared dream world. The "clothes that react to emotions" is a brilliant game mechanic—it turns vulnerability into strength. The search for the boy feels like a search for a missing piece of herself, a connection to a reality she's only glimpsed in dreams. The carnival setting, with its mixture of learning "crypto" and "smoking weed," gives it a very distinct, almost ritualistic coming-of-age vibe. She's not just a protagonist; she's a puzzle herself, decoding her own existence while decoding ciphers.
